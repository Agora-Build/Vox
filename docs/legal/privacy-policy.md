# VOX — Privacy Policy

**Last updated: July 10, 2026**

## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how the VOX project ("VOX", "we", "us") collects,
uses, discloses, and safeguards information when you use the VOX website,
applications, and APIs (the "Service"). VOX is a **community-driven, open-source
project** that evaluates conversational and real-time AI products and publishes
the results.

By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in
accordance with this Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

## 2. Scope

This Policy applies to information processed through the hosted VOX Service. It
does **not** apply to:

- third-party products and services that we evaluate or integrate with (each has
  its own privacy practices); or
- separate deployments of the open-source VOX software operated by third parties
  (self-hosted instances are controlled by whoever operates them).

## 3. Information We Collect

We aim to collect only what is needed to operate the Service.

**a. Information you provide:**

- **Account information** — such as your email address and display name.
- **Authentication information** — if you sign in with a third-party provider
  (for example, Google or GitHub), we receive basic profile information (such as
  your email, name, and identifier) from that provider. Passwords, where used, are
  stored only as salted cryptographic hashes.
- **Organization information** — organization names and membership, where you use
  team features.
- **Evaluation content** — workflows, eval sets, configurations, and related
  settings you create.
- **Secrets and credentials** — values you supply so we can run the evaluations
  you request. These are stored in encrypted form and used only to perform the
  evaluations you initiate.
- **Communications** — information you provide when you contact us.

**b. Information collected automatically:**

- **Usage and evaluation data** — records of evaluations you run and their
  results, job metadata, and activity within the Service.
- **Technical data** — IP address, browser/device information, timestamps, and
  server logs, used for security, rate limiting, debugging, and reliability.
- **Cookies and similar technologies** — see Section 6.

**c. Payment information:**

If you purchase paid features, payment is processed by a third-party payment
processor. We do **not** store full payment card numbers; we may retain limited
billing metadata (for example, a transaction identifier and status).

## 4. How We Use Information

We use information to:

- provide, operate, maintain, and secure the Service;
- authenticate users and manage accounts and organizations;
- run the evaluations you request and generate metrics, rankings, and the
  leaderboard;
- enforce rate limits, prevent abuse, and protect the integrity and security of
  the Service;
- process payments for paid features;
- diagnose problems, analyze usage in aggregate, and improve the Service; and
- comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Use.

We do **not** sell your personal information.

## 5. Legal Bases for Processing

Where applicable law (such as the GDPR) requires a legal basis, we rely on:

- **Performance of a contract** — to provide the Service you request;
- **Legitimate interests** — to secure, maintain, and improve the Service, provided
  those interests are not overridden by your rights;
- **Consent** — where you have given it (which you may withdraw at any time); and
- **Legal obligation** — where processing is required to comply with the law.

## 6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use strictly necessary cookies to keep you signed in and to maintain session
security. We may use limited functional cookies to remember preferences. You can
control cookies through your browser settings, but disabling strictly necessary
cookies may prevent parts of the Service from working.

## 7. How We Share Information

We share information only as follows:

- **Service providers** — with vendors who process data on our behalf to operate
  the Service (for example, hosting/infrastructure providers, authentication
  providers, and payment processors), under obligations to protect the data.
- **Public evaluation results** — evaluation metrics and rankings that you choose
  to make public, or that are part of the public leaderboard, are visible to
  others. Public results relate to evaluated products and test conditions; you
  control the visibility of the workflows and eval sets you create.
- **Legal and safety** — where we believe disclosure is reasonably necessary to
  comply with law, enforce our Terms, or protect the rights, property, or safety
  of the project, our users, or the public.
- **Project transfers** — in connection with a transfer of stewardship of the
  project, consistent with this Policy.

We do not sell personal information or share it for cross-context behavioral
advertising.

## 8. Third-Party Services

The Service relies on third parties, which may include authentication providers
(such as Google and GitHub), a payment processor, and hosting/infrastructure
providers. These parties process information under their own privacy policies. We
encourage you to review them.

## 9. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed
to provide the Service, and thereafter only as necessary to comply with legal
obligations, resolve disputes, enforce our agreements, and maintain the integrity
of published evaluation history. Aggregated or de-identified data, and published
evaluation results tied to products and test conditions (rather than to you), may
be retained on an ongoing basis.

## 10. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information,
including hashing of authentication tokens and passwords and encryption of stored
secrets. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and
we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for keeping your
credentials and API keys confidential.

## 11. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

- access, correct, or update your personal information;
- request deletion of your personal information;
- object to or restrict certain processing;
- request a portable copy of information you provided; and
- withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

You can update or delete much of your information through your account settings.
To exercise other rights, contact us using the details in Section 15. We may need
to verify your identity before acting on a request, and some information may be
retained where permitted or required by law.

## 12. International Data Transfers

The Service may be operated from, and information may be processed in, countries
other than your own, where data-protection laws may differ. Where required, we
take steps to ensure appropriate safeguards for such transfers.

## 13. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal
information from anyone under 16 years of age. If you believe a child has provided
us with personal information, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps
to delete it.

## 14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be indicated by
updating the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, by additional
notice. Your continued use of the Service after the changes take effect
constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.
